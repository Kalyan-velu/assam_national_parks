3. Dibru - Saikhowa National Park

See More...

It is a National Park as well as a Biosphere Reserve having an area of 340 sq. kms. in Tinsukia district. An unique habitat endemic to Dibru-Saikhowa only, a habitat which has undergone radical transformation after the great earthquake of 1950. The migratory birds are a major attraction.
  It have a tropical monsoon climate with a hot and wet summer and cool & dry water. It mainly consists of moist mixed semi-evergreen forests, canbrakes, grasslands. It is the largest salix swamp forest in NortEast India.
Dibru-Saikhowa National Park is framed for its Feral horses and many species like- Tiger, elephants, leopard, barking deer, water buffalos etc.
