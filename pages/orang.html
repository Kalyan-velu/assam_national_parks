5. Orang National Park and Tiger Reserve (ONPTR)

See More...

The oldest game reserve of the State just on the northern bank of river Brahmaputra with an area of 78.80 sq. kms. and an important breeding ground for varieties of Fishes. The Orang National Park in Assam has been officially renamed as Rajiv Gandhi Orang National Park. In 1915, Orang forest was declared as a Game reserve and it was later declared as a national park in 1999.
At one time, Orang was one of the finest representations of natural wet, allurial grasslands of floodplains of Brahmaputra river. Even now, more than 60% of the Park is under grass.
Orang National Park is an important habitat for he Indian one-horned Rhinoceros and Tigers.
