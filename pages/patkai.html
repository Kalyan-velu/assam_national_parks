7. Dihing-Patkai National Park

See More...

Dihing patkai National Park is located in the Dibrugarh and Tinsukia district. It was declared as a Wildlife sanctuary on 13 June 2004.
On 9 June 2021 forest Department of Assam officially notified it as a national park.
  The Dihing patkai forest region has a rich cultural heritage. There are more than a dozon different ethnic groups living in the area including the indigenous Assamese communities.
Dihing -Patkai rainforest harbours about 293 birds species, belonging to 174 general and 51 families. Rare animals are found here, such as Chinese pangolin, flying fox, serow, wild pig, sambar & barking deer.
It is the only National Park in India that houses Seven different species of cats- tiger, leopard, clouded leopard, leopard cat, dolden cat, jungle cat and marbled cat.
